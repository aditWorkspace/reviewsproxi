## Trigger Map

| Trigger Type | Specific Conditions |
|---|---|
| **Converts when** | App works reliably without crashes for extended testing period / Clear, transparent pricing with no hidden subscription requirements / Strong customer support responsiveness during trial period |
| **Abandons when** | App crashes frequently or becomes unstable during initial use / App updates causing functionality to break unexpectedly / Missing essential UI elements like basic navigation or input fields |
| **Trusts when** | Accurate product descriptions match actual app performance / Clear device/OS compatibility information provided upfront / Transparent privacy policies with specific data usage explanations |
| **Suspicious when** | Apps automatically restart or cannot be turned off / Inconsistent feature availability across multiple accounts / Apps require permissions that seem unrelated to functionality |

### Deal Breakers (Immediate Exit)

- 🚫 App crashes or becomes unusable after updates
- 🚫 Lost data due to phone crash despite multiple backup setups
- 🚫 Being locked out of account without ability to verify or log in
- 🚫 Apps that require constant in-app purchases or subscriptions to access content
- 🚫 Clear evidence of algorithmic control limiting expression or reach

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

Common mistakes when designing experiences for this demographic:

- ⚠️ Assuming they want cutting-edge features - they prioritize stability over innovation (contradicts Technical Reliability Concern)
- ⚠️ Pushing beta or experimental features - they explicitly avoid unstable software (contradicts Critical Reliability Failure pattern)
- ⚠️ Using vague privacy language - they specifically seek transparency about data usage (contradicts Platform Distrust pattern)
- ⚠️ Emphasizing automatic updates as a benefit - they want control over when changes happen (contradicts update-breaking functionality fears)
