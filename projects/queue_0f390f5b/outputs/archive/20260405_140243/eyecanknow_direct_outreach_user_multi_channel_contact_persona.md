## Trigger Map

| Trigger Type | Specific Conditions |
|---|---|
| **Converts when** | Clear, transparent pricing with no hidden fees is prominently displayed / Free trial or money-back guarantee reduces purchase risk / Recent positive reviews confirm reliable software performance |
| **Abandons when** | Pricing requires signup or contact sales to view / No customer support contact information is visible / Recent reviews mention crashes, bugs, or reliability issues |
| **Trusts when** | Support team responds quickly to pre-sale questions / Pricing is clearly displayed with no hidden fees mentioned / Product works immediately without installation problems |
| **Suspicious when** | Marketing claims seem too good to be true compared to user reviews / Pricing structure is complex with multiple hidden upgrade requirements / Customer support is unresponsive or provides incorrect information |

### Deal Breakers (Immediate Exit)

- 🚫 Software that crashes or has critical bugs preventing core functionality
- 🚫 Complete absence of accessible customer support channels
- 🚫 Hidden costs or surprise fees not disclosed upfront
- 🚫 Products that don't work on their required devices or operating systems
- 🚫 No refund policy for non-functional products
- 🚫 Unresponsive customer support when technical issues arise
- 🚫 Software that behaves like malware or is difficult to uninstall

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

Common mistakes when designing experiences for this demographic:

- ⚠️ Assuming they'll tolerate technical issues - they have zero patience for buggy software due to client stress (contradicts Support and Stability Frustration cluster)
- ⚠️ Hiding pricing behind forms - their high price sensitivity and low trust means they'll abandon immediately (contradicts Reliability & Transparency cluster)
- ⚠️ Pushing complex features - they want simple solutions due to time constraints from client demands (contradicts Professional Client Management cluster)
- ⚠️ Minimizing support importance - they view responsive support as essential, not optional (contradicts Consumer Rights Advocate cluster)
- ⚠️ Using high-pressure sales tactics - their pragmatic approach means they'll research thoroughly regardless of urgency claims (contradicts Neutral Pragmatist cluster)
