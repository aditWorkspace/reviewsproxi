## Trigger Map

| Trigger Type | Specific Conditions |
|---|---|
| **Converts when** | Detailed warranty coverage addresses their specific concern / Multiple reviews confirm long-term reliability for their exact use case / Clear installation guide shows compatibility with their setup |
| **Abandons when** | Product descriptions use vague or misleading language / No clear path to customer service or warranty claims / Installation requires professional help or permanent modifications |
| **Trusts when** | Warranty terms specifically address common failure modes / Customer service responds quickly to pre-purchase questions / Product specifications include compatibility with older systems |
| **Suspicious when** | Shipping protection isn't offered or costs extra / Return policy has complex requirements or fees / Product claims seem too good for the price point |

### Deal Breakers (Immediate Exit)

- 🚫 No clear return policy or restocking fees mentioned
- 🚫 Vague product descriptions that could be misleading
- 🚫 Customer service only available through automated systems
- 🚫 Products requiring professional installation without warning
- 🚫 No warranty coverage for common failure modes mentioned in reviews

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

Common mistakes when designing experiences for this demographic:

- ⚠️ Assuming they want the cheapest option - they prioritize reliability over low price due to repeated failure experiences
- ⚠️ Using high-pressure sales tactics - their low trust starting point makes them suspicious of urgency claims
- ⚠️ Hiding customer service information - their past poor support experiences make contact accessibility crucial
- ⚠️ Focusing on features over durability - they care more about long-term performance than impressive specifications
- ⚠️ Minimizing installation complexity - their preference for easy setup stems from past frustration with complicated installations
