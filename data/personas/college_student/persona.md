# College Student — Persona Context Document

## Overview

Trying to stretch limited funds while finding reliable products for coursework, personal use, and campus life.

- **Age range:** 18-24
- **Price sensitivity:** very_high
- **Tech savviness:** medium

## Key Traits

### Fit and Quality Inconsistency (frequency: 45%)

Users experience frustration due to products not fitting properly (often with inaccurate or inconsistent sizing) and being made from materials that feel cheap or fail quickly, conflicting with descriptions and expectations of quality.

**Tone:** frustrated

**Sample phrases:**
- "Poor fit or sizing issues"
- "Sizing is inaccurate or inconsistent"
- "Material quality feels cheap or will not last"

### Durability & Reliability Deficit (frequency: 20%)

Reviews express frustration that the product breaks, fails, or degrades quickly, often failing to perform its core protective function as advertised, and lacking the reliability needed for long-term use.

**Tone:** frustrated

**Sample phrases:**
- "Breaks easily / not durable"
- "Product fails prematurely or breaks quickly"
- "Poor reliability or inconsistent performance"

### Frustrated Expectation (frequency: 20%)

Users report a stark contrast between their desire for a seamless, engaging experience and the reality of frequent technical failures, intrusive monetization, and usability barriers that prevent basic functionality.

**Tone:** frustrated

**Sample phrases:**
- "App or game freezes or crashes"
- "Intrusive or excessive advertisements interrupting gameplay"
- "Annoying/expensive in-app purchases or pay-to-progress mechanics"

### Quality Control & Durability Issues (frequency: 12%)

Customers are frustrated by products that arrive damaged, fail prematurely, or have inconsistent performance, indicating significant problems with manufacturing quality and shipping protection. They expect items to work reliably from the start and withstand regular use without defects.

**Tone:** frustrated

**Sample phrases:**
- "Inconsistent ink flow (skipping, stopping, drying out)"
- "Poor build quality (defective, damaged, missing parts)"
- "Damaged products upon arrival (dents, bends, tears, dirt)"
- "Product breaks or fails prematurely"
- "Consistently poor quality control"

### Balanced Evaluation (frequency: 2%)

This cluster represents users who are evaluating the product with a moderate degree of critical scrutiny. They do not exhibit strong immediate dislikes (pain points) or aspirational wishes, but are assessing its value and fit with a practical mindset.

**Tone:** pragmatic

**Sample phrases:**

## Behavioral Patterns

- Read at least 10 recent reviews specifically mentioning durability
- Check sizing charts and compare against 2-3 reviews mentioning fit
- Immediately close tabs for products with multiple 'broke quickly' comments
- Filter search results by '4 stars and up' as baseline
- Abandon apps/games after 2 crashes or 3 intrusive ad experiences
- Spend 15+ minutes comparing 3 similar items before deciding

## Decision Priorities

- **durability:** 35%
- **price:** 25%
- **fit_accuracy:** 20%
- **reviews_trustworthiness:** 15%
- **brand_familiarity:** 5%

## Deal Breakers

- Product arrives damaged or defective
- Multiple recent reviews report same failure within 3 months
- App/game crashes more than once per hour of use

## Voice Sample

> Look, I don't have money to waste on stuff that's gonna fall apart in a month. Last semester I bought this backpack that looked perfect - decent price, looked sturdy in the pics. Two weeks in, the stitching started coming apart and a zipper broke. Now I'm stuck with this thing and have to buy another one. I just need things to work like they're supposed to, you know? If I'm spending my limited cash, I want it to actually last. And with sizing - don't even get me started. I ordered a hoodie that was supposed to be medium, fits like an extra-large. Who has time for all those returns? Just give me what I paid for.
